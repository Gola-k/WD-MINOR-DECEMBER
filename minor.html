Historical Places Of India


Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum located on the south bank of the Yamuna River in the Indian city of Agra.

Red Fort is a historical fort in the city of Delhi in India, built during the Mughal Empire in the 17th century.

Harmandir Sahib, also known as the Golden Temple, is a Gurdwara located in the city of Amritsar, Punjab, India.

Qutub Minar is a minaret that forms part of the Qutb complex, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the Mehrauli area of Delhi, India.

The Ajanta Caves are caves in Maharashtra, India, dating from the 2nd century BCE to about 480 or 650 CE.

The Ellora Caves are located in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra, India. They are one of the largest rock-cut monastery-temple cave complexes in the world.
